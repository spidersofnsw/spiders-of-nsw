The Grey Wolf Spider

Body length:
-Male: 18mm
-Female: 25mm

Portacosa cinerea is a large burrowing wolf spider which has the unique attribute of being one of the only wolf spider species to make trapdoors over their burrows. They like to burrow in hard, dry and compact clay as this keeps their trapdoor stable and secure. The burrow itself is a circular hole in the ground which can go up to 20cm deep. There is a small side chamber at the bottom where the spider resides. Portacosa cinerea is very abundant in temperate to semi-arid envoirments, while it seems to avoid damper enviorments. The trapdoor is often very hard to spot as it can look like cracks in the clay. Often, the spider will use things such as rocks, leaves and grass to help camoflage the burrow, making them even harder to spot. They are easiest to find after dark, when they open their burrows to go hunting.

The male (top right corner) was found at Webbs Creek NSW. The burrows were found at Hazlett Park Macquarie Fields.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Underside is cream, light brown or a mix.
-Has uniform grey carapace.
-Smaller juveniles can have slight paternation on abdomen.
-Black chevron at the start of the abdomen.
