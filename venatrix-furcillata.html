Striped Wolf Spider

Body length:
-Male: 10mm
-Female: 13mm

Venatrix furcillata is a species of small wolf spider. It makes a simple burrow in damp soil, but is mostly free roaming. This species is extremely common and ubundant in the temperate reigons of NSW, and can be found in forests, parks and farmland across the state. This species can occasionally migrate in large communal groups, which is quite unique for wolf spiders. The bite of this spider is painful, but it harmless to humans.

The Male (top left corner) was found at Riverview NSW.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Has a fully black underside, with yellow lines on the abdomen.
-Has a thin band around the carapace.
-Has teardrop shaped pattern on the abdomen facing the carapace.
-Has a white line going down the carapace.
