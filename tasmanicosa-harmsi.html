Tasmanicosa harmsi is a large, inland wolf spider. It is a species which is often mistaken with another species, Tasmanicosa ramosa, due to similarities in habitat and appearance. This species also makes a burrow which can have a lid, but is more unlikely than Tasmanicosa ramosa. Most commonly, Tasmanicosa harmsi makes a circular burrow which goes down vertically, sometimes at mild angles. This species has 'variants' depending on the location the spider is found in. For example, a South Australian variant from Woomera frequently makes a trapdoor, but is more dull in colouration compared to the variant which frequently makes open burrows.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Has slight blue tinge on the legs (from the tip to halfway up)
-Species has different 'variants' (can vary in appearance and behavior)
-Burrow can be open or lidded
