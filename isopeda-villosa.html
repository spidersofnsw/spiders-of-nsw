Sydney Grey Huntsman

Body length:
-Male: 25mm
-Female: 38mm

Isopeda villosa is a large and very common huntsman spider found along the coast of NSW under the bark of eucalyptus trees, but it is also very frequently encountered in homes. The bite of this spider, although largs, lacks anything more than localised pain. Some say the bite is similar to the sting of a European Honey Bee (Apis mellifera). Females make a chamber under bark when making an eggsac by lining a layer of silk all arounnd her, covering any openings for protection of her young.

The female and male (top row) were both found at Wategora Reserve NSW.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Has a pale venter.
-Has no banding on legs.
-Has 4-6 spots on the abdomen.
