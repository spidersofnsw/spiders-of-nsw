Banded Wolf Spider

Body length:
-Male: 22mm
-Female: 32mm

Tasmanicosa ramosa is a large species of inland dwelling wolf spider. This species is often mistaken with another local species, Tasmanicosa harmsi, which inhabits the same habitat, distribution and appearance. This spider is called the "Banded" wolf spider due to the 'W' shaped stripes which are on the abdomen. In juveniles, the distinct patternation is more subtle and harder to notice. The spider makes burrows which can have a lid.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Has distinct banding on abdomen
-Has Union-jack pattern on carapace
-Burrow can be open or lidded
