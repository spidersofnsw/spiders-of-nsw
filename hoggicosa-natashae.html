Giant White Tiger Wolf Spider

Body length:
-Male: 25mm
-Female: 35mm

Hoggicosa natashae is potentially the biggest wolf spider on Australia! With a body length of 35mm and a legspan of almost 80mm. This species is found in the arid reigons of NSW, but can be found in other states. It makes a lidded burrow in desert sandy clay soils, and it's lid is often extremely hard to spot when closed. This species doesn't make a very deep burrow, only around 15cm deep. The habitat this species inhabits is quite harsh, and often very hot, so having a lid is necessary to prevent the spider from drying out.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Has distinct patternation on carapace and abdomen
-Species may cluster (There can be multiple in close proximity)
