Spotted Huntress

Body length:
-Male: 10mm
-Female: 12mm

Venatrix pictiventris is a small wolf spider which inhabits eucalypt forest and is very common in leaf litter. These spiders often make short burrows under small rocks as a shelter during the day, or when the spider is moulting, otherwise the spider is mainly free roaming. Venatrix pictiventris is called the Spotted Huntress due to the patterns on the underside of the abdomen, which are small white spots. This is a feature which helps tell apart another similar species (Venatrix pseudospecicosa) which otherwise looks the same. It has a painful bite, but is harmless to humans.

Male Venatrix pictiventris (top right corner) was found at Wonboyn NSW. Venatrix pictiventris (bottom left corner) was found at Somersby NSW.

*IMPORTANT CHARECTARISTICS*

-Has white spots on the underside of the abdomen.
-Has black underside on carapace.
-Has central band going across the carapace.
